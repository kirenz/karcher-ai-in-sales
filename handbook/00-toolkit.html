5  Your AI toolkit: four tools

Four tools sit behind this whole training. We introduce each tool one at a time below, and you meet it again later at the exact point in the cycle where it is first needed.

  • Gemini drafts from scratch; a Gem is Gemini after one good briefing (you get one ready-made per step).
  • Some Gems run on Deep Research — Gemini’s mode for going out, searching public sources, and citing them — so they research before they draft.
  • NotebookLM works only from the sources you give it and shows the source — it can answer, summarise, or build a table or briefing you can trace back to the page.
  • Workspace Studio turns a task you keep repeating into a standing routine — when X arrives, do Z — that still waits for your approval before anything sends.
  • You feed a Gem by attaching a Google Doc, then export its answer as a new Doc, a chain across the cycle.

5.1 Gemini and Gems

Gemini is the general-purpose assistant: you type a request and get a draft back. Think of it as a new colleague, fast and capable, who knows nothing about your work, so you brief them from scratch every time.

A Gem is that same colleague after one good briefing. You load in your best instructions and a few reference documents once, and it sticks: from then on you just name the account and it already knows how we work. You do not build these yourself; you receive one ready-made Gem for each step of the cycle.

Some of those Gems do their best work by researching first — going out to the public web, pulling current facts, and citing them — before they draft. The mode that does that is Deep Research, and because we lean on it so often it has its own page.

→ Learn more about Gemini and Gems on the Gems page.

→ The research mode several of these Gems run on has its own page: Deep Research.

5.2 NotebookLM

NotebookLM works only from the documents you give it, and nothing else. Think of it as an expert who has read exactly those sources. Ask a question and the answer comes straight from those pages, with the source shown.

And it does more than answer: from the same sources it can summarise them, pull the facts into a table, or draft a briefing or even a presentation, each grounded in what you uploaded.

That source-anchoring is what makes it the natural home for the reliability questions from the survey: you get outputs you can trace back to the page, not confident guesses.

→ Learn more about NotebookLM on the NotebookLM page.

NoteGem vs NotebookLM

A Gem works from everything it knows, while NotebookLM stays inside the documents you give it and shows its source.

5.3 Workspace Studio

Workspace Studio turns a task you keep repeating into a standing routine that runs on a trigger — when an email from X about Y arrives, do Z — so the work happens without you starting it by hand.

It is no-code: you describe the trigger and the action in plain language, and the result still waits for your approval before anything sends.

→ Learn more about Workspace Studio on the Workspace Studio page.
