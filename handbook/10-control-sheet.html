29  The Leadership Control Sheet

The leading artifact for the whole leadership track: one Google Sheet that holds the pipeline, the risks, the coaching notes, and the next decisions. It is the Cluster-B counterpart to the seller’s NorthStar account.

This is the page to read before the three Gem tiles, because all three run on this one sheet. The Leadership Track overview explains the altitude shift; this page makes the working data concrete.

29.1 Your NorthStar is a whole portfolio

Across the cycle, Cluster A spends the day reasoning about one account. For the leadership track we widen the lens. Your working data is not a single case but a portfolio: NorthStar sits inside it, alongside five to eight further example accounts, all in one pipeline view, on the same data everyone else in the room is using.

That matters because steering decisions are comparative by nature. You cannot decide which account deserves the team’s Monday by looking at one row; you decide it by looking across the whole list, comparing potential against potential and risk against risk. So the leadership exercises all run on this small portfolio, the same way Cluster A’s exercises all run on NorthStar. Same data discipline, wider frame.

NoteGive the Gem only the fields it needs — the portfolio is the safe ground

The shared seminar portfolio is made up for the training, so you can prompt with it freely. When you later point these Gems at your own real pipeline, the Module 0 data rules apply: in our approved tools you may work with the real pipeline. As good practice, give the Gem only the fields the task actually needs. To rank opportunities the model needs stage, value, and dates — not a customer’s phone number. Less data is both sharper and tidier, and you still verify the output.

29.2 What the sheet holds

This is the leading artifact for the whole track, the Cluster-B counterpart to the seller’s NorthStar account, so it is worth being concrete about what it is. The Leadership Control Sheet is a single Google Sheet — nothing more exotic than that. It is your cockpit: what NorthStar is for a seller, this sheet is for you. It holds four things:

  • Pipeline and segments — every account in one place, so the whole book can be ranked rather than reviewed account by account.
  • Risks — the deals that need a human decision, made visible instead of buried in the middle of a list.
  • Coaching notes — what each team member needs, kept next to the numbers that justify it.
  • Next management decisions — the real output of steering work, written down rather than carried in your head.

Everything the three leadership Gems produce lands back here. For a seller, the artifact that travels through the cycle is the NorthStar account; for a leader, the artifact that travels through this track is this sheet.

29.3 The whole data model is a flat list

A common worry at this level is that steering with AI needs a data warehouse or heavy new tooling. It does not. The Control Sheet is a flat list, one row per account, with a handful of columns you can already maintain: account and segment, stage and value, last contact, and a short note. The last-contact date is the single most useful field, because it lets the model see which accounts have gone quiet. That is the whole data model, and it is enough for Gemini to read the entire portfolio and surface the three signals that matter for steering:

  • Stage aging — which deals have sat too long in one stage. Long aging is not always bad, but it often signals a stalled deal, unclear ownership, or low urgency.
  • Momentum — which accounts are moving and which have gone quiet. A good account with low momentum still needs a nudge, even when the value looks attractive.
  • Whitespace — where there is unworked room to grow. In the Kärcher context this usually means service opportunity, rollout potential, or installed-base potential that has not yet been turned into an active motion. It is so often hidden service revenue.

There is one signal worth checking before any clever ranking: the follow-up gap, the accounts with no recent contact and no defined next step. They are not lost, they are simply forgotten, and they leak revenue quietly. Asking the model to list every account that has gone quiet without a plan is often the fastest win available in a given week.

With the sheet in place, the three Gem tiles each read one part of it: the Pipeline-Steering Gem ranks the whole book, the Forecast Gem reads the closing outlook, and the Coaching & Steering Gem reads the team-KPI tab.
