8  See Your Pipeline at a Glance

Where does the money sit? With your accounts in a Sheet, one prompt turns the list into a compact overview — pipeline value by stage and by segment — without a single formula.

  • One prompt creates a pivot table on its own new tab — pipeline value by stage and by segment.
  • “Use only what is in my data” keeps the numbers grounded in your sheet.
  • Verify one total by hand before you act on the overview.

8.1 One prompt, one new tab

Gemini creates the summary on a new tab by itself — nothing to insert, nothing to place. If it shows a small plan with an Approve button first, just approve it. Then open the new “pipeline value” tab at the bottom of the sheet: your pivot table is there.

TipPipeline at a glance 
What is my total pipeline value by stage and by segment? Give me the result as a new table in a new tab called pipeline value. Use only what is in my data.

No sheet of your own yet? Use the practice tracker (File → Make a copy) — it has stages, segments and values ready to summarise.

8.2 Trust it, then verify one total

  • Pick one small segment and add its values by hand — does the total match?
  • Read the assumptions it states (excluded rows, currencies) and confirm the ones that matter.
  • The table says where the money sits — what you do about it stays your call.

The full Sheets page is Google Sheets. The sales version of this move — ranking your own accounts to decide where the week goes — is Pipeline analysis.
