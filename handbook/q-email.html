1  Reply to a Customer Email

A reply is sitting in your inbox and you cannot find the right words. This is the fastest win in the whole toolkit: Gemini sits inside Gmail, you give it a short brief, and you get a draft you only have to read, fix, and send. Nothing goes out on its own.

  • Open the email, open the Gemini panel (top right), and paste the brief — there are no blanks to fill.
  • Gemini reads the open email and interviews you for the one thing only you know: what you want to say.
  • Read it once before you send: the draft saves the blank page, not the judgment.

1.1 What you need

  • The email — the message open in Gmail, or the practice email below.
  • Your facts — what you want to say, in a few plain words. Gemini asks; you answer.

1.2 Let Gemini interview you

Paste and run — nothing to fill in:

TipThe reply brief 
Role: You are a helpful assistant who writes clear, friendly customer emails.
Task: Help me reply to a customer email. First interview me, one question at a time: what is the customer asking (read the open email, or ask me to paste it), and what do I want to say. Then write the reply.
Context: I work in sales at Kärcher, the cleaning-technology company.
Format: Three short paragraphs, friendly and professional, no jargon.
Check: List anything you assumed about the customer or the product so I can confirm it before I send. Do not state any specs, prices, dates, or commitments I did not give you.

1.3 No email at hand? Practice on this one

A made-up customer inquiry — copy it and paste it when Gemini asks for the customer’s email.

From: Sandra Willems, Site Manager, Van Dalen Facility Group
Subject: Machine demo next week — three questions

Dear Mr. Bauer,

Thank you for your visit last week. Before we confirm the demo, three questions:

1. Could we move the demo from Tuesday to later in the week? Our cleaning team lead is back from leave on Thursday and should really see it.
2. Is there an operator guide in Dutch we could look at beforehand?
3. Does the demo include a short introduction for our cleaning team, or is that a separate booking?

Best regards
Sandra Willems
Site Manager, Van Dalen Facility Group

Your facts for the reply: the demo can move to Thursday next week at 10:00; an operator guide in Dutch exists and you will attach it; a short on-site introduction for the cleaning team is part of every demo.

1.4 Read it once before you send

The draft saves you the blank page, not the judgment. Three checks, ten seconds:

  • Does it say what you actually want to say — and does the tone sound like you?
  • Did it invent a detail you did not give it — a date, a spec, a promise? Fix it or cut it.
  • Look at the assumptions Gemini listed and confirm the ones that matter.

Do this often? Build yourself a helper Gem with fixed instructions — see Gems. The full Gmail page, including thread summaries, is Gmail.
