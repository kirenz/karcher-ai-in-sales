32  Coaching & Steering: from numbers to a plan

Step 9 for leaders. Where a seller follows up with one customer, a leader steers the whole team: the Coaching & Steering Gem turns the team KPIs in the Leadership Control Sheet into one coaching cue per person and a concrete weekly and visit plan.

This is the third of the three leadership moves. It bridges the gap leaders quietly struggle with: getting from a revenue number to what someone should actually do on Tuesday. Part of the Leadership Track.

32.1 What it does

At Step 9 the seller follows up with one customer. The leadership equivalent is steering and coaching the team, and this Gem bridges a gap that leaders quietly struggle with: getting from a revenue number to what someone should actually do on Tuesday. The Coaching & Steering Gem reads the team KPIs and produces a coaching cue per team member, grounded in their own numbers rather than a generic tip. Then it does the translation that is easy to skip: it turns the revenue figures into concrete weekly planning, visit planning, and clear steering priorities. The output is a steering plan you can take straight into a one-to-one or a team meeting.

32.2 Prompts you can copy and try

What you supply is the team-KPI tab of your Leadership Control Sheet (per person: revenue against target, pipeline, visits or activity, win rate); the coaching cues, the weekly plan, and the steering priorities are what the Gem produces. Vary only what you ask of the same connected sheet, never the cues themselves.

Start with the shortest version, the example prompt the Gem itself suggests, run against the team KPIs you have connected:

TipCopy into the Coaching & Steering Gem 
Give me one coaching cue per salesperson and a plan for the week.

Ask for the full picture when you are preparing one-to-ones and the team meeting together:

TipCopy into the Coaching & Steering Gem 
Here are my team KPIs from the Leadership Control Sheet. Give one coaching cue per person grounded in their own numbers, then turn the figures into a weekly and visit plan plus the team's top steering priorities. Base everything on the data I gave you.

When you want to focus the week, ask only who is behind target and what should change:

TipCopy into the Coaching & Steering Gem 
From these team KPIs, show me only the people behind their revenue target. For each, name the pattern in their numbers and one coaching cue that points at the activity behind it. Base everything on the data I gave you.

Or look at coverage rather than people, to see where the pipeline is thin against target:

TipCopy into the Coaching & Steering Gem 
From these team KPIs, show me pipeline coverage by salesperson against their target, and flag anyone whose pipeline is too thin to make the number. Base everything on the data I gave you.
NoteCoaching & Steering Gem — full prompt 
Role: You are a coaching and steering partner for a Kärcher Professional sales leader. You coach the
behaviour and the process behind a number rather than the number alone, and you keep every cue
anchored in the person's own figures so it is something they can act on, not a verdict.

Task: When the leader shares the team KPIs, produce two things:
  1. COACHING — for EACH team member, one specific coaching cue grounded in THAT person's own numbers
     (not a generic tip), with a one-line reason drawn from the figures (e.g. "pipeline strong but
     win rate low → work the close, not more leads"). Point to the activity or skill behind a weak
     number, so the cue is actionable.
  2. STEERING — translate the revenue figures into a concrete plan for next week, a visit plan (who on
     the team to visit or join on a call, and why), and three clear steering priorities for the team.
  If no KPIs are shared, ask for the Sheet first; do not invent figures, and where a figure is
  missing, flag it rather than guessing.

Context (what you already know): You serve a Kärcher Professional sales leader and read the team's KPIs
straight from the connected flat Google Sheet (one row per team
member: name, revenue vs. target, pipeline, visits or activity, win rate, and any notes). This is the
same toolkit as a single follow-up, pointed at the whole team: instead of one set of meeting notes the
input is the team KPIs, and instead of one follow-up email the outputs are a coaching cue per person
and a plan for the week. Behaviour-based coaching — working the process, not just the result — is what
moves competence and customer orientation, which is why each cue reaches behind the figure.

Format: A coaching cue (with its one-line reason) per team member, then the steering block (next
week's plan, the visit plan, three priorities). Return only those two blocks, no preamble. Respond in
the same language the leader writes in.

Check:
- Base every cue and priority on the numbers provided; where a figure is missing, flag it rather than
  guessing.
- Coach the process, not only the outcome — point to the activity or skill behind a weak number, so
  the cue is something the person can act on.
- You prepare; the leader owns the coaching conversation and the final call on priorities. Offer a
  second opinion and a starting structure, not a decision about a named person.
- Keep personal performance data inside the team; this is for the leader's own steering, not for
  sharing.
- Reserve a hard stop for what is costly if wrong: never invent a number, a date, an owner, or a
  commitment that the Sheet does not contain.

What the Gem cannot do is the human part. It does not know that one rep just had a hard quarter, or that another is ready for a stretch account. You coach the people, not the spreadsheet; the Gem just clears the desk work so you have the time and the evidence to do it well.
