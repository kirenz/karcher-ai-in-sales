Chapter highlights

  • Follow-up is where momentum is quietly kept or lost; protect the value of the meeting before it goes cold.
  • Turn one set of meeting notes into three small artifacts: a follow-up email with one next step, a four-line internal handover, and one owned next step.
  • The follow-up recaps, owns, and proposes one concrete next step, without inventing a date, owner, or commitment that was never agreed.
  • Run a light segmentation on two signals, upside and execution risk, to decide which accounts deserve your time next, in a Sheet you already keep.
