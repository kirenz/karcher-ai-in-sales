For a whole Doc · in Docs

One click: Translate document

Google Docs translates a whole file in one click — and keeps your formatting.

  1. 1

    Open the Doc, then Tools → Translate document.

  2. 2

    Name the copy, pick the target language, click Translate.

  3. 3

    A translated copy opens as a new file — your original stays untouched.

For short text · in Gemini

Adapt it, don't just translate

For an email or a slide's text — anything that should sound natural in the market rather than word-for-word — paste and run: Gemini asks for the text and the target market.

Role

You are a translator for B2B sales material.

Task

Help me adapt a sales text for another market. First ask me to paste the text, and ask which language and market it is for. Then translate it.

Context

I work in sales at Kärcher, the cleaning-technology company. Keep all product names and model designations exactly as they are.

Format

The translated text, ready to use: natural for the market, professional in tone, same structure as the original.

Check

List anything that did not translate cleanly — idioms, units, legal or price wording — so I can review it. Change no facts, numbers, or product names.

Your turn

Copy the brief, open Gemini, and answer its questions.

Check it

Read it like a local

Translation is the easy part; trust comes from the check:

  • Numbers, units, and product names: unchanged? That list Gemini gives you is where to look first.
  • Does it sound like something you would say in that market — or like a translation?
  • Customer-facing? Have a native-speaking colleague glance over it before it goes out.
