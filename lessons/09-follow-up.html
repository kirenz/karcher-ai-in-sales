Follow-up & Steering

Protect the value of the meeting: follow-up email, internal handover, owned next step, and a light account segment.

The idea

Protect the meeting's value

The work is done, but the deal is not. A quick, clear follow-up is what keeps it moving: recap, agree one next step, and send it within a day.

  • Follow-up is where momentum is quietly kept or lost; protect the value of the meeting before it goes cold.
  • Turn one set of meeting notes into three quick things: a follow-up email, a four-line handover for colleagues, and one clear next step that someone owns.
