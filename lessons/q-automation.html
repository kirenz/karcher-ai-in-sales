First, the split

One email, or the same email every week?

One-off · Gmail

A single email that needs an answer now: draft it with Gemini in Gmail. That is the Reply card.

Recurring · Studio

The same kind of email, again and again: describe the routine once in Workspace Studio, and it runs every time.

A flow has three parts: a trigger ("when an email like this arrives…"), an action ("…do this"), and your decision at the end. The Chat message arrives on its own — but this starter flow never writes to the customer.

Do it · in Workspace Studio

Describe it, don't program it

You don't click the flow together. Describe the whole task in one prompt — Gemini builds the flow, and you adjust the steps afterwards.

Copy-paste prompt:

When a new email from a customer arrives in my inbox, classify it as a quote request, a complaint, an order-status question, or "other / unclear", and send me a message in Google Chat with the category and a one-line summary. Do not reply to the email.

  1. 1

    Open Workspace Studio, paste the description into "Describe a task for Gemini", and click Create.

  2. 2

    Review the steps Gemini built — edit, reorder, or add what is missing.

  3. 3

    Keep the decision with you: the flow notifies — it never replies to the customer on its own.

  4. 4

    Use the flow's built-in Test run to see what it would do, then turn it on — and check the first real Chat messages against what you would have said.

Check it

Automate after the habit, not before it

The rule that keeps flows useful, as three checks:

  • Is the manual routine already clean and repeatable? Automating a messy process just makes the mess faster.
  • In the first days: do the categories match what you would have said? If not, sharpen the trigger and action wording.
  • One-off mail stays in Gemini in Gmail — a flow is only for the routine that repeats.
