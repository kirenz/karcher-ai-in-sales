Prompting

A weak answer usually means a weak brief, not a weak AI.

What it is

Talk to AI like a new assistant

How do you actually talk to an AI? Picture a sharp new assistant on their first day: capable, but it knows nothing about you or your customer, and it cannot read your mind, only your brief.

Give it a clear instruction with the background only you have and you get usable work; a vague one-liner gets a generic reply.

The five parts

A good brief has five parts

So what makes a brief clear? Five parts cover it. You will not need all five every time; it is a checklist, not a form.

  • Role: the perspective it writes from.
  • Task: the one output you want.
  • Context: the facts only you know.
  • Format: length, structure, and tone.
  • Check: ask it to list its assumptions, so you know what to verify.

See it work

Weak brief, strong brief

Meet Martin Bauer, a Kärcher sales rep we'll follow as our running example. He wants to find out where AI could save him time, so watch his request go from weak to strong.

Weak

“How can I use AI in my sales job?”

No role, no context, no check. Gemini returns a generic brochure that could fit anyone, and Martin still does the real thinking.

Briefed · the five parts

Role

You are an AI-productivity coach for B2B field sales.

Task

Give me a short, prioritized list of concrete ways to use Gemini in my sales week.

Context

I'm Martin Bauer, Kärcher Professional sales in Germany. My week is account research, meeting prep, follow-up emails, and proposals. New to AI.

Format

A compact table, one row per use case: task · how it helps · what to watch. About eight rows, most time saved first.

Check

List the assumptions about my role and tasks I should verify.

Same question, five parts filled in, and Gemini comes back with a prioritized shortlist Martin can act on, not a generic brochure.

Level up

Show it an example

One more move sharpens a brief fast: show the AI an example of a good answer. Paste a follow-up email you were happy with, or one strong use case, and tell it to match that style. It copies the pattern from your example better than from any description.

Level up

Attach a document, don't paste

Your Context is often a whole document: rough notes, a proposal, a product sheet. Don't paste a wall of text. In Gemini, click the + at the bottom-left of the message box and pick 'Add from Google Drive' (or 'Upload files'). Gemini reads the whole document as context, so the answer is built on your facts, not its guesses.
