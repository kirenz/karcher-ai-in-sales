Catch up on a long email thread

Twenty replies, five minutes before the call. Get the state of things in five bullets.

You have

A long, tangled email thread you have no time to re-read.

You’ll create

Five bullets: what has been decided, what is still open, and who owes the next step.

Time

About 3 minutes, right inside Gmail.

What you need

Just a thread from your inbox

A long thread of your ownAny busy conversation from your inbox — a scheduling back-and-forth, a quote clarification. Everyone has one; that is your practice material.

Do it · in Gmail

Ask Gemini, not your memory

Two clicks, one paste:

Copy-paste prompt:

Summarise my last email thread in five bullets: what has been decided, what is still open, and who owes the next step.

  1. 1

    In Gmail, click the Gemini icon at the top right — the chat panel opens on the right.

  2. 2

    Paste the prompt into the chat and run it. Have a specific conversation open? Say "this email thread" instead of "my last email thread".

Open Gmail ↗

Even faster · in Gemini

Don't even open the thread

Gemini is connected to your Gmail: you can ask about your mail without finding the thread first. Replace [name] with the person or company and run it in Gemini.

Copy-paste prompt:

Summarise my recent emails with [name or company] in five bullets: what has been decided, what is still open, and who owes the next step.

Open Gemini ↗

Check it

Scan it against the mail once

A summary you act on deserves ten seconds of doubt:

  • Do the "decided" points match your memory? Spot-check one against the original mail.
  • Asked across your mailbox? Glance at which emails it pulled — the right thread, the full picture?
  • Note who owes the next step. If that is you, that is your to-do.

Done

✓

Task done

You produced: The state of a twenty-reply thread, in five bullets you checked.
