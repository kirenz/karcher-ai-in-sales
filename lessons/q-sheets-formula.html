Let Gemini write the formula

The part of Sheets most people dread — handed over in one plain sentence.

You have

A sheet, and a thing you want counted or summed.

You’ll create

A working formula, plus where to put it — pasted and done.

Time

About 3 minutes, right inside Sheets.

What you need

A sheet and a plain sentence

Your sheetThe table open in Google Sheets — or your copy of the practice tracker.
What you want countedSay it in plain words — no formula vocabulary needed.

No sheet of your own yet?

Use the practice tracker (File → Make a copy) — the prompt below counts its "Proposal Sent" accounts.

Open the practice tracker ↗

Do it · in Sheets

Say what you want, get the formula

Ask in plain words. Gemini returns the formula and tells you where to put it — paste it into the cell.

Same move for sums: "Write a formula that sums the value of all accounts in the 'Negotiation' stage."

Copy-paste prompt:

Write a formula that counts how many accounts are in the "Proposal Sent" stage.

Open Google Sheets ↗

Check it

Paste it, then sanity-check once

A formula you did not write still deserves one look:

  • Count or add the rows by hand once — does the formula's result match?
  • If the cell shows an error, paste the error message back to Gemini and let it fix the formula.
  • Formulas live: when your data changes, the number updates on its own — that is the point.

Done

✓

Task done

You produced: A working formula in your sheet — written by Gemini, checked by you.
