Kärcher · Quick help

Research a customer

A company name becomes a briefing: who they are, what they care about, how Kärcher could help.

You have

A company name and their website — nothing else.

You’ll create

An account briefing: who they are, what they care about, and a first idea of how Kärcher could help.

Time

About 5 minutes of your time — the research then runs a few minutes on its own.

What you need

One line is enough

Company name + websiteThat is all you give it. For practice, pick any real company you know.

Do it · with Deep Research

Copy-paste prompt:

Build an account briefing for [company name] ([website]) in preparation for a Kärcher Professional sales conversation.

Check it

A briefing is a hypothesis

Deep Research reads the public web — so read the briefing like a colleague's draft:

  • Click two or three of the cited sources — do they actually say what the briefing says?
  • Facts about size, sites, or plans: treat them as to-verify until a source confirms them.
  • The "how Kärcher could help" idea is your starting hypothesis for the first conversation, not a fact.
