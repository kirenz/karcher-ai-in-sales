Gemini & Gems

The difference between a blank chat and a briefed assistant.

What it is

A Gem is Gemini after one good briefing

Gemini is the general-purpose assistant in your Kärcher Workspace: type a request, get a draft. A blank Gemini chat is a new colleague, useful but it knows nothing about your work, so you brief it from scratch every time. One setting matters from day one: in the model picker at the top, choose the Thinking model — it takes a moment longer and gives noticeably better answers. Make it your default.

A Gem is that same colleague after one good briefing: instructions and reference documents are loaded in once, and they stick.

How you use them

You open ready-made Gems

For this training you never build a Gem. Each step already has one prepared for you.

  • You open a ready-made Gem per step, straight from a link we give you.
  • It comes already briefed on how Kärcher works.
  • Feed it a document (usually a Google Doc), not a wall of pasted text.

Where they live

Finding your Gems

Want to browse your Gems or find one again? Click the gear at the bottom-left of Gemini and choose Gems. In the training you'll usually open the right one straight from a link, so no hunting needed.

Optional · for the curious

Build your own Gem

You won't need this for the training, every Gem is prepared for you. But if you'd like to build one, here's a simple, useful example, a Sales Email Helper. Copy each part into the Gem builder:

  1. 1

    Name it and add a short description.

    Name

    Sales Email Helper

    Description

    Turns my rough notes into a clear, friendly customer email.

  2. 2

    Instructions: set its role and how it should work, the same five-part brief from Prompting.

    Instructions

    Role: You are an experienced B2B sales assistant who writes clear, friendly, professional customer emails. Task: Turn my rough notes into a short, ready-to-send email. Context: I sell Kärcher professional cleaning equipment to business customers. Keep the tone warm but concise, and never invent facts, prices, or product details I did not give you. Format: A short email with a subject line, a greeting, two to four short paragraphs, and one clear next step. Plain business English. Check: If anything important is missing, such as the customer name, product, or deadline, ask me before writing.

  3. 3

    Knowledge: skip it for this simple one; add reference files only if a Gem should always use them.

  4. 4

    Save, and open it any time from your Gems list.
