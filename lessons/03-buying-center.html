Contacts & First Contact

In business, several people decide together. First you map who they are and what each one cares about, then you write one email to open a conversation with the right person.

The idea

It's never just one person

In business, you rarely sell to just one person. A handful of people shape the decision, and each cares about something different: does it deliver the result, is it worth the cost and risk, does it make daily work easier? This group of deciders is called the buying center, and your first job is to map it, so you know who they are and what each one wants.

  • Your Buying Center Map has one row per person: what they want, what worries them, how best to approach them, and the risk of leaving them out. Treat every entry as a guess to confirm in the conversation, not a settled fact.
  • Then you write one short email to the right person, usually the decision-maker, to open a conversation, not to pitch. It aims to win a meeting, not to close the deal.

Quick check

In business, who usually decides on a purchase like new cleaning machines?

Your first move

Why you open with Lars

Of the four, you reach Lars Meijer first. As Regional Operations Director he owns the outcome your pilot promises, uptime and a smooth rollout, so he is the one who can say yes to a first step. You bring the others in later; right now you open the door with the decision-maker.
