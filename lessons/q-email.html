Reply to a customer email

Turn the email you are staring at into a reply you only have to check and send.

You have

A customer email in your inbox that needs an answer, and the facts for the reply in your head.

You’ll create

A reply draft written with Gemini — read, fixed, and sent by you.

Time

About 5 minutes, right inside Gmail.

What you need

Two things, nothing more

The emailThe message open in Gmail — or the practice email on the next screen.
Your factsWhat you want to say, in a few plain words. That is the one part only you know.

Practice material

No email at hand? Use this one

A made-up customer inquiry — copy it and paste it when Gemini asks for the customer's email.

Your facts for the reply: the demo can move to Thursday next week at 10:00; an operator guide in Dutch exists and you will attach it; a short on-site introduction for the cleaning team is part of every demo.

Practice email

From: Sandra Willems, Site Manager, Van Dalen Facility Group Subject: Machine demo next week — three questions Dear Mr. Bauer, Thank you for your visit last week. Before we confirm the demo, three questions: 1. Could we move the demo from Tuesday to later in the week? Our cleaning team lead is back from leave on Thursday and should really see it. 2. Is there an operator guide in Dutch we could look at beforehand? 3. Does the demo include a short introduction for our cleaning team, or is that a separate booking? Best regards Sandra Willems Site Manager, Van Dalen Facility Group

Do it · in Gmail

Let Gemini interview you

Open the email in Gmail, open the Gemini panel (top right), and paste the brief — there are no blanks to fill. Gemini reads the open email (or asks you to paste one) and asks what you want to say. For the practice email, answer with the facts from the previous screen.

Role

You are a helpful assistant who writes clear, friendly customer emails.

Task

Help me reply to a customer email. First interview me, one question at a time: what is the customer asking (read the open email, or ask me to paste it), and what do I want to say. Then write the reply.

Context

I work in sales at Kärcher, the cleaning-technology company.

Format

Three short paragraphs, friendly and professional, no jargon.

Check

List anything you assumed about the customer or the product so I can confirm it before I send. Do not state any specs, prices, dates, or commitments I did not give you.

Your turn

Copy the brief, open Gmail, and answer its questions.

Check it

Read it once before you send

The draft saves you the blank page, not the judgment. Three checks, ten seconds:

  • Does it say what you actually want to say — and does the tone sound like you?
  • Did it invent a detail you did not give it — a date, a spec, a promise? Fix it or cut it.
  • Look at the assumptions Gemini listed and confirm the ones that matter.
