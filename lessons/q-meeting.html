Kärcher · Quick help

Get ready for a customer meeting

Walk in with likely topics and good questions — prepared in the time of a coffee.

You have

A meeting coming up, and a rough idea of who the customer is.

You’ll create

Five topics the customer is likely to raise, and five good questions to ask.

Time

About 5 minutes, in Gemini.

What you need

Three answers in your head

Just your answersWhich company, who you are meeting, and what the meeting is about. Gemini asks — you answer in plain words. Nothing to fill in.

No meeting this week?

Answer the interview with the practice case: Van Dalen Facility Group — Sandra Willems, Site Manager — a machine demo next week.

Do it · in Gemini

Let Gemini interview you

Paste and run — there are no blanks to fill. Gemini asks you three quick questions (company, person, occasion), and builds your prep from the answers.

Role

You are a customer-meeting prep assistant.

Task

Help me prepare for a customer meeting. First interview me: ask me one question at a time — which company I am meeting, who the person is and their role, and what the meeting is about. Then prepare me.

Context

I work in sales at Kärcher, the cleaning-technology company.

Format

After my answers, give me (1) five topics the customer is likely to raise, and (2) five good questions I should ask. Short bullet points.

Check

Flag any point that assumes a fact I did not give you, so I can check it before the meeting.

Your turn

Copy the brief, open Gemini, and answer its questions.

Check it

Keep what fits, drop the rest

The list is raw material, not a script:

  • Keep the topics that fit this customer; drop the generic ones without guilt.
  • Pick the two or three questions you would actually ask — and make them yours.
  • Look at the flagged assumptions: anything worth checking before the meeting?

Done

✓

Task done
