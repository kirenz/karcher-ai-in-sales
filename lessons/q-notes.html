Turn meeting notes into a follow-up

Rough bullets from the visit become a tidy note the customer actually gets.

You have

Rough notes from a customer meeting — bullets, fragments, half sentences are fine.

You’ll create

A short follow-up note to the customer: what was agreed, one clear next step — read and sent by you.

Time

About 5 minutes, in Gemini.

What you need

Just your notes

Your rough notesWhatever you scribbled during or after the meeting. Gemini asks you to paste them — no tidying first.

Practice material

Do it · in Gemini

Let Gemini interview you

Paste and run — there are no blanks to fill. Gemini asks for your notes (and for the customer, if the notes do not say), then writes the follow-up.

Role

You are an assistant who turns rough notes into a tidy customer follow-up.

Task

Help me follow up after a customer meeting. First ask me to paste my rough notes, and ask who the customer is if my notes do not say. Then write the follow-up.

Context

I work in sales at Kärcher, the cleaning-technology company.

Format

A short follow-up note to the customer: a greeting, a two-sentence recap of what was agreed, the open points, and one clear next step.

Check

List anything you inferred from my notes, and do not add prices, specs, or promises I did not write.

Your turn

Copy the brief, open Gemini, and paste your notes when it asks.

Check it

Your notes, nothing more

The note goes to a customer — so check it like one:

  • Does the recap match what actually happened in the meeting — nothing added, nothing dropped?
  • Look at what Gemini says it inferred: promises, dates, or specs you did not write get cut.
  • Is the next step the one you want? Then make the tone yours and send it.
