Build a presentation from scratch

Describe the update; Gemini drafts brand-matched slides you then make yours.

You have

A blank deck and a message in your head.

You’ll create

A first draft: five slides with a clear arc — situation, change, impact, recommendation, next step.

Time

About 5 minutes, right inside Google Slides.

What you need

The topic, in two sentences

Your topicWhat the update is about and what you want the audience to take away. Two sentences are enough.

No update to build right now?

Answer the interview with the practice topic: a pilot of a new cleaning machine at a customer site — first results are positive, and your recommendation is to extend the pilot to a second site.

Do it · in Slides

Let Gemini interview you

In a blank deck, click Ask Gemini (top right) and paste the prompt — there are no blanks to fill. Gemini asks what the update is about and what the audience should take away, then drafts the slides.

Copy-paste prompt:

Create 5 slides for a short internal update with this arc: the situation, what changed, the impact, the recommendation, and the next step. First ask me, one question at a time: what is the update about, and what should the audience take away? Then build the slides. Use only rectangular shapes — no rounded corners.

Open Google Slides ↗

Check it

A draft, not a deck

Generated slides look finished before they are. Before you present:

  • Does each title state the answer, not the topic? Rewrite the ones that just name a theme.
  • Kärcher image rules: no AI-generated product shots or infographics — use real product and brand material, and label AI-generated media for internal use.
  • Brand check: graphic elements are rectangular — fix any rounded corners.
  • Every fact on the slides came from you — nothing added? Cut what you did not say.

Done

✓

Task done

You produced: A five-slide first draft with a clear arc — finished by you, not by the model.
