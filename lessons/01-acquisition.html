Acquisition

Use AI to become the visible expert in your industry, so customers come to you. Or use AI to find the accounts worth approaching.

The idea

Two roads to a lead

Two roads to a lead: inbound (be found as an expert) and outbound (source a shortlist).

  • Acquisition creates a lead where none exists yet. Two roads: inbound (be found) and outbound (go find them).
  • Inbound means posting useful industry insight on LinkedIn, so people come to you as an expert instead of you cold-calling them.

Quick check

Which post earns inbound attention?

The first road

Be the expert they find

Inbound · now

Post useful insight, become the visible expert, and let the right customers come to you.

Outbound · later

The other road, coming up after: go find accounts to approach yourself.

The next two recipes walk this road: build one industry briefing for your own use, then turn it into a stream of short posts.

Do it · Recipe 1 of 3

Draft the internal briefing

A briefing on your target industry, for your own use: its trends, pressures, and priorities, so you sound credible and have things worth posting. You keep it; you do not publish it.

What you give itYour target industry and the priority segment you want to win.

Copy-paste example prompt:

Research the BSC (Building Service Contractors) industry for me, focused on facility-services providers in the Benelux.

Open the Industry-Research Gem ↗
Create a Google DocExport it to a Google Doc in your Drive, for example 'BSC Briefing'.Then: You attach that Doc in the next recipe to turn it into posts.

The second road

Or go find accounts yourself

Inbound · done

Recipes 1 and 2 were the inbound road: build a presence and let the right customers come to you.

Outbound · now

This is the outbound road: name a market and let the Lead-Finder surface accounts to approach.

The two roads are independent, so this one does not build on your posts. Most reps use both, and both end the same way: a named account you research in Step 2.

Do it · Recipe 3 of 3

Find accounts to approach

A ranked, sourced shortlist of candidate accounts in a market you choose, the outbound route to a lead.

What you give itThe market or segment you want to source in.

Copy-paste example prompt:

Find candidate accounts in the BSC (Building Service Contractors) industry in the DACH region.

Open the Lead-Finder Gem · Deep Research ↗
Create a Google DocExport it to a Google Doc, for example 'BSC Target Shortlist'.Then: Pick one account and take its name and website into the Account-Briefing Gem in Step 2.
