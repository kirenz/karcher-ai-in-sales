Research & Qualification

A lead is just a company name so far. Here you research it, then decide one thing: is this account worth pursuing?

The idea

The goal is a decision

Researching a lead is not about collecting facts. It is about answering one question: is this account worth your time? A yes is a ‘Go’, a no is a ‘No-Go’, and either answer is a win, because it sends your effort where it pays off.

  • What the research finds from public sources is a starting guess, not proof. You confirm it later, in the real conversation with the customer.
  • A prepared Gem does the digging for you and searches many public sources, so your effort goes into the judgment: which facts to trust, and whether to pursue the account.

Quick check

The research says the account has budget approved this quarter. Before you have spoken to them, that is…
