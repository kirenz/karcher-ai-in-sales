Find insights in a sales Sheet

Ask your account list a plain question and get a readable answer. No formulas.

You have

A Google Sheet with your accounts or deals — or the ready-made practice tracker.

You’ll create

A plain answer to a question like "which accounts need my attention?", grounded in your own data.

Time

About 5 minutes, right inside Sheets.

What you need

A table and a question

Your sheetA table with your accounts: one row per account, columns like stage, value, last contact, next step.
Your questionWhat you want to know, in normal words — the way you would ask a colleague.

No sheet of your own yet?

Use the ready-made practice tracker: twelve fictional accounts with stages, values and next steps. Open it, then File → Make a copy, and work on your copy.

Open the practice tracker ↗

Do it · in Sheets

Ask your data a plain question

Open your sheet and click Ask Gemini (top right). Then ask in normal words. The phrase "use only what is in the sheet" keeps the answer grounded in your data instead of a confident guess.

Same move, other questions: "What is my pipeline value by segment?" — "Which deals have been stuck in the same stage the longest?"

Copy-paste prompt:

Here is my account sheet. Which accounts have had no contact in the last three weeks and have no defined next step? Use only what is in the sheet.

Open Google Sheets ↗

Check it

AI sorts, you decide

A wrong answer sends your attention to the wrong place. Treat the answer as a hypothesis you confirm, not a verdict:

  • Does the list match what you know about these accounts?
  • Read the assumptions it states — for example, which date it treated as "today" — and correct them if they matter.
  • Spot-check one or two rows in the sheet before you act on the list.

Done

✓

Task done

You produced: A short list of the accounts that need your attention, straight from your own sheet.
