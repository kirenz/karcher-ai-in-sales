Chapter highlights

  • The close is a step, not a trick: read the buying signal, choose a path, capture the result. Optional by design, so it stays short.
  • Judge the signal's strength and match it: a gentle hypothetical close for a soft signal, a direct close when scope and timing are clear.
  • Treat an objection as information, classify first, then answer calmly; meet a price objection in three moves (understand, reframe to operating cost, check), never defend the number.
  • Capture a post-meeting summary that keeps agreed and open strictly separate; the AI never invents a commitment, owner, or deadline.
